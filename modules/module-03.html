Section 1

Background & Story

How machine learning came to be

Training a machine learning model is like teaching someone a new skill through practice and feedback. You provide examples, the model learns patterns, and you evaluate its performance. The key is finding the right balance between learning the training data and generalizing to new, unseen data.

Modern tools and frameworks have made it easier than ever to train your own models, even without deep mathematical expertise.

Historical Timeline

1950
The Turing Test
Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test as a measure of machine intelligence, asking whether a machine could exhibit behavior indistinguishable from a human.
1959
Birth of Machine Learning
Arthur Samuel coined the term "machine learning" while developing a checkers-playing program that improved through self-play.
2012
Deep Learning Revolution
AlexNet won the ImageNet competition with deep convolutional neural networks, demonstrating the power of deep learning and sparking the modern AI boom.
Section 2

Core Concept

What machine learning really means

Definition

Model training is the process of feeding data to a machine learning algorithm so it can learn patterns and relationships. During training, the model adjusts its internal parameters to minimize the difference between its predictions and the actual outcomes.

Key Vocabulary

Common Misconception

Higher training accuracy doesn't always mean a better model. If a model performs perfectly on training data but poorly on new data, it has overfit. The goal is generalization, not memorization.
